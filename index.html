Hi there! I am a dual-title PhD student at the University of Washington in Oceanography and Astrobiology.

My primary research field is chemical oceanography, meaning I'm interested in how the chemistry of seawater impacts ocean and climate health. Specifically, I research how organic molecules affect trace metals in the ocean. Trace metals like iron, manganese, and copper are in very low concentrations in the global ocean but are vital nutrients for microbial life. Organic molecules called ligands affect their distribution and bioavailability by binding to these metals. Microbes under severe metal limitation will even produce special chelators called siderophores with an extremely high binding strength to iron â€” strong enough to dissolve minerals! Thus, studying metal-ligand dynamics and characterizing ligands like siderophores are of great importance to understanding biogeochemical cycling and marine life. My work on ligands has spanned hydrothermal vents and seamounts, phytoplankton blooms in the Southern Ocean, the Equatorial Pacific HNLC, and even whale poop. From an astrobiological perspective, researching microbe-chemistry interactions in Earth's ocean will help us understand and evaluate potential life on other ocean worlds like Enceladus.
